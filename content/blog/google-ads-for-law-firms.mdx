## Key Takeaways

- **Budget allocation should mirror case value:** personal injury campaigns deserve 60-70% of total Google Ads spend because a single signed case worth $50,000 to $500,000 justifies CPCs of $150 to $300, while estate planning cases at $3,000 to $8,000 average value cannot absorb those same click costs profitably.
- **Smart Bidding outperforms manual CPC at scale:** once a campaign accumulates 30+ conversions monthly, Target CPA bidding reduces cost per lead by 18-22% according to [Google's best practices documentation](https://support.google.com/google-ads/answer/7065882).
- **Cost per signed case is the only metric managing partners need:** Google Ads typically delivers signed cases at $1,000 to $3,000, Local Service Ads at $500 to $1,200, and mature SEO at $250 to $500 per case.
- **Multi-location firms should use shared budgets below $50K monthly spend:** separate campaigns per office create budget fragmentation that starves high-performing locations.
- **Quality Score directly controls your CPC:** WordStream's legal industry data shows that improving Quality Score from 5 to 8 reduces actual CPC by up to 37% on identical keywords.

## Budget Allocation by Practice Area

The biggest mistake law firms make with PPC advertising for law firms is treating all practice areas equally in budget allocation. Case economics should dictate spend. Personal injury, with average case values between $50,000 and $500,000+, justifies aggressive CPCs because the return on a single signed case covers months of ad spend. According to [WordStream's legal advertising benchmarks](https://www.wordstream.com/blog/ws/2017/07/17/legal-advertising), the average CPC for personal injury keywords sits between $150 and $300, while family law averages $80 to $150, criminal defense runs $50 to $120, and estate planning keywords cost $30 to $80.

A multi-practice firm spending $30,000 monthly should allocate approximately:

- **Personal injury:** $18,000 to $21,000 (60-70%)
- **Family law:** $4,500 to $6,000 (15-20%)
- **Criminal defense:** $3,000 to $4,500 (10-15%)
- **Estate planning:** $1,500 to $3,000 (5-10%)

These ratios shift based on your firm's capacity constraints and signed case targets. If your PI attorneys are at capacity, redistribute to growth areas.

## Smart Bidding Strategies for High-CPC Legal Keywords

Manual CPC bidding made sense when legal CPCs averaged $20 to $50 per click. With google ads for law firms now costing $150 to $300 per click, the stakes of each auction demand algorithmic precision. Google's Smart Bidding strategies (Target CPA, Maximize Conversions, and Target ROAS) use machine learning to adjust bids across device, location, time, audience, and dozens of other signals in real time.

The critical threshold: campaigns need at least 15 conversions per month for Target CPA to function, though stability improves dramatically above 30 conversions monthly. For a law firm Google Ads strategy, this means:

- **Months 1-3 (learning phase):** Use Maximize Clicks with a bid cap or manual CPC. Build conversion history by tracking form fills, phone calls (via CallRail, CallTrackingMetrics, or similar), and live chat requests.
- **Months 3-6 (transition):** Switch high-volume campaigns to Maximize Conversions. Let the algorithm learn your conversion patterns.
- **Month 6+ (optimization):** Move to Target CPA once you have reliable cost per lead benchmarks. Set targets 10-15% below your current average to push efficiency without starving volume.

For personal injury firms where a single case justifies enormous spend, Target ROAS bidding is powerful once you can feed signed case values back into Google Ads through offline conversion imports.

## Multi-Location Campaign Structure

Multi-location law firms running google ads for law firms face a structural decision that directly impacts performance: shared budgets across offices or dedicated campaigns per location. The right answer depends on total monthly spend and how different each market is.

**Shared budget approach (recommended under $50K/month):**

Run one campaign per practice area covering all locations. Use geographic bid adjustments (+20% for high-performing metros, -30% for lower-value suburbs) and location-specific ad copy through ad customizers. This prevents budget fragmentation where your Tampa office starves while Jacksonville has unused daily budget.

**Dedicated campaigns per location (recommended above $50K/month):**

Each office gets its own campaign set with independent budgets, bid strategies, and landing pages. This works for firms like Morgan & Morgan, Cellino & Barnes, or other [personal injury lawyer marketing](/blog/personal-injury-lawyer-marketing) operations running six or seven figures monthly. The advantage is granular control; the disadvantage is management complexity.

Regardless of structure, every location needs its own call tracking number, Google Business Profile, and location extensions. Google Ads management for lawyers at scale requires attribution clarity between offices.

## Common Google Ads Mistakes Law Firms Make

After auditing hundreds of google ads for law firms accounts, these errors appear repeatedly and silently drain budgets:

**1. Broad match keywords without negative keyword lists.** Running "car accident lawyer" on broad match triggers searches like "how to become a car accident lawyer" or "free car accident lawyer" that will never convert. Build negative keyword lists with 200+ terms from day one. Review search term reports weekly.

**2. No practice-area level tracking.** Lumping all campaigns into one budget and one conversion action makes it impossible to know which practice areas generate positive ROI. Separate conversion actions per practice area, even if campaigns share budgets.

**3. Ignoring Quality Score.** Google rewards relevant, well-structured campaigns with lower CPCs. A Quality Score of 8+ means you pay less per click than competitors bidding more. Focus on ad relevance (keyword-to-ad copy match), landing page experience (fast, mobile-friendly, matching search intent), and expected CTR (compelling headlines with USPs).

**4. Sending traffic to the homepage.** Every ad group needs a dedicated landing page matching the keyword intent. "Car accident lawyer Houston" should land on a Houston-specific PI page with client results, not a generic homepage. This single change can improve conversion rates by 30-50%.

**5. Not importing offline conversions.** When running google ads for law firms, the algorithm optimizes toward whatever conversion you track. If you only track form submissions, Smart Bidding optimizes for form fills regardless of lead quality. Import signed cases as offline conversions so the algorithm learns what a good lead actually looks like.
