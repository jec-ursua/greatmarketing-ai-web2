## PI-Specific Targeting That Actually Works

Facebook ads for personal injury lawyers succeed or fail on targeting precision. Under Meta's Special Ad Category for legal services, you lose access to detailed demographics like income and net worth. What remains is still powerful when used correctly.

**Geographic radius targeting** is your strongest lever. Build custom audiences with 10-15 mile radii centered on Level I trauma centers, urgent care clinics, chiropractic offices, and high-volume auto body shops in your jurisdiction. According to the [National Highway Traffic Safety Administration's 2023 crash data](https://crashstats.nhtsa.dot.gov/Api/Public/ViewPublication/813561), 72% of injury crashes occur within 15 miles of the victim's home, meaning your geo-fence captures the majority of potential clients.

Layer age targeting (21-54, the peak MVA demographic per [IIHS fatality data](https://www.iihs.org/topics/fatality-statistics/detail/yearly-snapshot)) on top of geographic radius. Add interest categories for auto insurance, health and wellness recovery, and legal services. Build lookalike audiences from your CRM's signed-case list; even a seed of 100 past PI clients gives Meta enough signal to find similar profiles.

## Compliant Ad Copy for State Bar Rules

Every state bar regulates attorney advertising differently, but the universal prohibitions for PI lawyer Facebook ads are consistent: no guarantees of outcomes, no misleading claims about expertise, and mandatory disclaimers on past results.

**Required elements for most jurisdictions:**

| Element | Example Language |
|---------|----------------|
| Attorney Advertising disclaimer | "Attorney Advertising. Prior results do not guarantee a similar outcome." |
| No guarantee language | Avoid "We will win your case" or "Guaranteed settlement" |
| Past results disclaimer | "Case results depend on individual facts. Past verdicts and settlements do not guarantee future results." |
| State-specific identifier | California: "Rafael Hernandez, licensed in CA." Florida: include bar number |

The American Bar Association's [Model Rule 7.1](https://www.americanbar.org/groups/professional_responsibility/publications/model_rules_of_professional_conduct/rule_7_1_communications_concerning_a_lawyer_s_services/) prohibits "false or misleading communication about the lawyer or the lawyer's services." For facebook advertising personal injury campaigns, this means your creative can reference settlement amounts only with disclaimers, and testimonials require disclosure that they represent one client's experience.

## Creative Formats That Convert for PI

The highest-performing creative formats for facebook ads for personal injury lawyers follow a pattern: social proof combined with urgency, wrapped in compliant framing.

**Video testimonials** (30-60 seconds) from past clients describing their experience generate the strongest engagement. Include a text overlay disclaimer ("Results may vary. Past case outcomes do not guarantee future results.") and keep the client's face visible for authenticity. According to [Meta's 2024 Creative Best Practices report](https://www.facebook.com/business/news/creative-best-practices), video ads in the legal category see 2.3x higher click-through rates compared to static images.

**Before/after case result graphics** work well when structured as: situation description (not medical images), then outcome. Example: "Maria was hit by an uninsured driver. Medical bills: $47,000. Settlement secured: $285,000." Always append the required disclaimer.

**Carousel ads** showing your process (free consultation, investigation, negotiation, settlement) reduce uncertainty for leads who have never hired an attorney. Each card answers a fear: "Will I pay upfront?" (No, contingency fee), "How long does it take?" (Most cases settle in 6-12 months). The landing page path allows you to pre-qualify with questions like "Were you the driver or passenger?" and "Did the accident happen in the last 30 days?" which your intake team needs before calling.

## Trusted Form and TCPA Compliance

Every PI lead generated through facebook lead ads personal injury campaigns must satisfy two compliance frameworks: the FCC's Telephone Consumer Protection Act (TCPA) and your state bar's advertising rules.

**TCPA requirements for PI lead ads:**

1. **Express written consent** before any call or text. Your lead form or landing page must include clear consent language: "By submitting this form, I consent to be contacted by [Firm Name] regarding my potential case."
2. **One-to-one consent.** Since the FCC's January 2025 one-to-one consent rule, leads cannot be sold to multiple firms unless the consumer explicitly selects each firm. This impacts any PI lead generation operation buying or selling leads.
3. **Trusted Form certification.** ActiveProspect's Trusted Form provides an independent, timestamped, video-recorded certificate of the consumer's form interaction. This is your evidence if a lead disputes consent or a bar investigator questions your advertising.

Implementing Trusted Form on Meta lead forms requires passing the certificate URL through a hidden field connected to your CRM via Zapier or a direct API integration. On landing pages, the JavaScript snippet fires automatically. Either way, the cost is negligible compared to the risk of a TCPA class action (statutory damages of $500-$1,500 per call).
