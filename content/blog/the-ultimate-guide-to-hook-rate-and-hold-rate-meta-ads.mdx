## What Is Hook Rate and Why It Matters

The hook rate is a simple yet powerful way to measure how effectively your video grabs attention.

Hook Rate Formula:

3-Second Video Views ÷ Impressions × 100

For instance, if your ad gets 10,000 impressions and 4,500 three-second views, your hook rate is 45%.

So, what is a good hook rate Facebook ads? Anything above 35% means your ad captures interest quickly. Between 25%–35% is decent but could use optimization. Below 25%? It’s time to rework your opening visuals or messaging.

If you’re working with a top [marketing agency in Los Angeles](https://greatmarketing.ai/), they’ll often prioritize testing hooks since this metric is a leading predictor of creative success.

## Understanding Hold Rate and Its Importance

While hook rate measures your ad’s first impression, hold rate reveals how well your creative keeps attention throughout the video.

Hold Rate Formula:

Through Plays ÷ 3-Second Views × 100

Example: If you have 4,500 three-second views and 2,000 through plays, your hold rate is 44%.

According to Facebook ad benchmarks, a good hold rate is above 50%. Between 40%–50% is average, while below 30% indicates people lose interest too quickly.

If you’re working with an experienced [advertising agency in California](https://greatmarketing.ai/), they’ll likely use hold rate to identify which creatives deserve scaling and which need refinement.

## How to Set Up Hook Rate and Hold Rate in Ads Manager

![social media manager tracking Facebook ads hook rate and hold rate metrics on computer](https://framerusercontent.com/images/Htz7Q2FI91WaONera94PPbfimQ.png)

The challenge? Facebook doesn’t show these metrics by default, you need to set them up manually as custom metrics.

Here’s how:

1.  Go to Columns → Customize Columns inside Ads Manager.
    
2.  Click Create Custom Metric.
    
3.  For Hook Rate, enter: `3-Second Video Views ÷ Impressions × 100`.
    
4.  For Hold Rate, enter: `Through Plays ÷ 3-Second Video Views × 100`.
    
5.  Set a goal (e.g., Hook Rate ≥ 35%, Hold Rate ≥ 50%).
    
6.  Save and add these custom metrics to your columns.
    

Once done, you’ll easily identify which ads have a strong opening (high hook rate) and which retain viewers (high hold rate).

## Benchmarking and Creative Testing Strategies

Tracking numbers is only half the battle, understanding how to _use_ them is where performance takes off.

The best creative performance analysis strategy is to test multiple versions of your video ads:

-   Film 3–5 different hooks for one video body.
    
-   Test two or more body variations with those hooks.
    
-   Compare results using hook rate and hold rate.
    
-   Identify your top performers and scale those versions.
    

This approach allows marketers to discover the best hook rate for Facebook ads by analyzing real audience behavior.

Working with [marketing agencies in Orange County](https://greatmarketing.ai/) can help refine this process through structured testing frameworks and advanced creative optimization tools.
